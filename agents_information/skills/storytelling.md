# Creator Skill — Storytelling (CEO Voice, Platform-Native)

## Skill Purpose
Turn an idea into a **story that carries a point**—without drama, fluff, or "influencer arcs."
CEO storytelling is not entertainment; it's **meaning + credibility**.

Use storytelling to:
- Make insights memorable
- Build trust through specificity
- Show how the CEO thinks, not just what they think

---

## When To Use
Use this skill when:
- The insight feels too abstract
- You need proof, texture, or stakes
- You want to humanize without oversharing
- The post requires narrative momentum (especially LinkedIn/IG)

---

## Inputs
You will receive:
- Selected idea + intended takeaway
- CEO Voice Profile (tone, boundaries, do/don't)
- Optional: a real incident, example, or customer moment
- Target platform(s): X, LinkedIn, Instagram

If no real incident is provided, create a story using:
- "A pattern I keep seeing…"
- "A conversation I had…"
- "A moment I realized…"
But keep it believable and general (no fabricated specifics like names/quotes).

---

## Non-Negotiables
1. **Point-first storytelling:** story exists to deliver a clear takeaway.
2. **Specificity without confidentiality:** avoid names, proprietary data, private details.
3. **No melodrama:** grounded, restrained, CEO-real.
4. **One story, one lesson:** don't mix multiple plotlines.

---

## The CEO Story Framework (Use This)
Choose the smallest narrative that carries the lesson:

1. **Context (1–2 lines)**
   - Where/when/what situation (minimal)
2. **Tension (1 line)**
   - What was hard, surprising, misunderstood
3. **Decision / Insight (1–2 lines)**
   - What changed the thinking
4. **Result (optional, 1 line)**
   - What improved (keep honest)
5. **Takeaway (1–2 lines)**
   - The principle others can apply
6. **Close (CTA)**
   - A question or reflection prompt

---

## Story "Ingredients" Toolkit
Pick 2–3 to add credibility:
- A constraint ("We had 2 weeks…", "We couldn't hire…") — only if true
- A tradeoff ("Speed vs quality…", "Growth vs focus…")
- A mistake ("I assumed…", "I missed…")
- A mental model ("Now I use this rule…") 
- A visible signal ("The meeting felt…", "The metrics showed…")

Avoid:
- "And then everything changed overnight"
- Overconfident outcomes
- Generic lessons without the moment that produced them

---

## Platform Adaptation

### X (Twitter)
**Goal:** Micro-story with a punchline insight.  
**Length:** 1–4 short lines (or 2–3 sentences).

**X Story Rules**
- Start as close to the tension as possible
- Cut context aggressively
- End with the lesson (or a question)
- No long setup, no multiple characters

**X Story Templates**
- "I used to do ____. It felt right. It was wrong.  
  What worked instead: ____."
- "Had a conversation today that reminded me:  
  ____ isn't the problem. ____ is."
- "A mistake I see a lot (including in myself):  
  ____. The fix: ____."

**X Output Requirement**
Provide:
- **1 primary X version**
- **1 alternate X version** (different opener)

---

### LinkedIn
**Goal:** Authority + trust through a real-seeming moment.  
**Length:** 6–14 short paragraphs, spaced.

**LinkedIn Story Rules**
- Hook first, then context
- Add 1–2 specific details that feel earned (not "viral")
- Keep the lesson concrete (a principle + how to apply)
- Close with a thoughtful question

**LinkedIn Story Templates**
- "I learned this the hard way.  
  [1–2 lines of what you believed]  
  [the moment it broke]  
  [the new model]  
  [how to use it]"
- "Behind the scenes of [building/leading]:  
  [tension]  
  [decision]  
  [result]  
  Here's what I'd do again: …"

**LinkedIn Output Requirement**
Provide:
- **1 primary LinkedIn version**
- **A stronger 'executive' variant** (more direct, fewer emotions)

---

### Instagram (Feed / Caption)
**Goal:** Relatable, saveable, shareable—still CEO-smart.  
**Length:** 8–20 short lines, mobile-first.

**Instagram Story Rules**
- Warmer tone than LinkedIn, but not "life coach"
- Use short lines and breathing room
- The takeaway should feel like something to remember
- Emojis optional (0–2) only if voice-aligned

**Instagram Story Templates**
- "A small moment I keep thinking about:  
  …  
  The lesson:  
  …"
- "I used to chase ____.  
  Now I optimize for ____.  
  Here's why: …"
- "If you're in the middle of ____, this helped me: …"

**Instagram Output Requirement**
Provide:
- **1 primary Instagram version**
- **A 'short caption' variant** (≤ 8 lines)

## Self-Check Before Hand-off
- ✅ Story supports one clear takeaway
- ✅ Sounds like CEO: grounded, precise, non-performative
- ✅ No confidential details or invented specifics
- ✅ Platform structure respected
- ✅ Close invites reflection (not engagement bait)

---

## What "Good" Looks Like
A good CEO story makes the reader think:
- "That's real."
- "That's specific."
- "That changed how I see it."

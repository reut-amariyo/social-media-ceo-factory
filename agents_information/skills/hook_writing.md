# Creator Skill — Hook Writing (Platform-Native)

## Skill Purpose
Write **high-performing, CEO-voice hooks** that earn attention without sounding clickbait-y.
A hook is the **first line(s)** that decide whether someone stops or scrolls.

This skill is used **before** drafting the full post:
Hook → Body → Close/CTA

---

## When To Use
Use this skill when:
- The idea is strong but the opening feels flat
- The post needs sharper positioning or tension
- You need multiple hook options to A/B test

---

## Inputs
You will receive (or infer from context):
- Selected idea + angle (from Ideator)
- Trend signal (from Scout, optional)
- CEO Voice Profile (mandatory constraint)
- Target platform(s): X, LinkedIn, Instagram

---

## Non-Negotiables
1. **No clickbait.** Curiosity must be earned by truth.
2. **CEO voice always.** Confident, clear, and specific.
3. **One hook = one promise.** Don't stack multiple claims.
4. **Specific beats clever.** Replace vague nouns with concrete ones.

---

## Hook Types (Choose 1–2 per post)
Pick based on the idea:

1. **Contrarian**
   - "Most people believe X. I think that's backwards."
2. **Hard-won lesson**
   - "It took me years (and mistakes) to learn this:"
3. **Specific observation**
   - "After [number] conversations with [audience], one pattern keeps showing up:"
4. **Myth → Reality**
   - "The myth: X. The reality: Y."
5. **Simple rule**
   - "If you remember one thing about [topic], remember this:"
6. **Unexpected tradeoff**
   - "Here's the cost of doing X that no one mentions:"
7. **Problem reframing**
   - "Your real problem isn't X. It's Y."
8. **Mini-story opener**
   - "Yesterday, something happened that reminded me why [belief]:"
9. **Strong opinion (with humility)**
   - "Unpopular take: X. (And here's why I'm convinced.)"

---

## Platform Rules (How Hooks Differ)

### X (Twitter)
**Goal:** Stop scroll in 1–2 lines.  
**Constraints:** Extremely tight. No setup. No fluff.

**X Hook Rules**
- 1 line preferred (max 2)
- Start with the **claim**, not the context
- Use line breaks for rhythm
- Avoid: "Thread:" unless CEO uses it naturally

**X Hook Templates**
- "Most advice about [topic] fails because it ignores one thing: ____."
- "If [audience] did ____ instead of ____, they'd get ____."
- "The biggest mistake I see in ____ is ____."
- "Unpopular take: ____."

**X Output Requirement**
Provide **5 hook options**, each ≤ 240 chars.

---

### LinkedIn
**Goal:** Win the "See more" click with credibility + curiosity.  
**Constraints:** First 2 lines are everything.

**LinkedIn Hook Rules**
- 1–2 short lines before "See more"
- Make a **clear promise**: insight, lesson, or model
- Strong voice, no hype words
- Avoid: "Here are 10 tips…" unless truly CEO-style

**LinkedIn Hook Templates**
- "I used to think ____ mattered most. I was wrong."
- "Most teams over-invest in ____. The leverage is in ____."
- "A lesson from building ____: ____."
- "The best founders I know do one thing differently: ____."

**LinkedIn Output Requirement**
Provide **5 hook options**, each as **2 lines max**.

---

### Instagram (Feed / Caption)
**Goal:** Make it feel human and shareable.  
**Constraints:** Mobile-first scanning; warmth without influencer tone.

**Instagram Hook Rules**
- 1 short line (or 2 very short lines)
- Slightly more emotional/relatable than LinkedIn
- Clarity > aesthetic text tricks
- Emojis optional (0–2) only if aligned with CEO voice

**Instagram Hook Templates**
- "No one tells you this about ____."
- "The moment I stopped ____, everything changed."
- "A simple rule that made ____ easier: ____."
- "If you're feeling stuck in ____, read this:"

**Instagram Output Requirement**
Provide **5 hook options**, each as **1–2 short lines**.

## Self-Check Before Hand-off
- ✅ Sounds like CEO (not marketer, not influencer)
- ✅ Specific nouns, verbs, claims
- ✅ Curiosity is earned, not manufactured
- ✅ Platform constraints respected
- ✅ No clichés ("game-changer", "crushing it", "hustle")

---

## What "Good" Looks Like
A great hook makes the reader think:
- "That's sharp."
- "That's true."
- "I need the rest of this."
